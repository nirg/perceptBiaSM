Call for Participation

The outcome of this workshop will be a position paper that frames research on social media systems and perceptual bias with respect to four research questions, given below.

  1. What are the key perceptual biases (e.g. stereotypes, ideologies, expectations) that impact social media systems?
  2. How do social media systems impact perceptual biases and how do the biases shape social media systems?
  3. How can social media data be used to complement existing methodologies (e.g. survey, lab experiments) for measuring specific perceptual biases?
  4. How can social media platforms be designed to reduce bias, if possible, in cases where it is undesirable?

In order to facilitate the creation of this position paper, we seek submissions that will assist in creating an outline for the position paper. All submissions should be less than a page and should contain two parts:

  1. A brief outline of three perceptual biases that impact the way one or more social media systems are used and/or that are impacted by the use of social media.
  2. A brief sketch of a potential project or an existing work that addresses Question 2, 3 or 4 from the above themes with respect to one or more social media systems.

Please sent your submissions to pbsm2017@gmail.com. The final position paper will be published in a venue chosen by workshop participants, and everyone who makes a submission and attends the workshop will be invited as a contributing author to the position paper.
